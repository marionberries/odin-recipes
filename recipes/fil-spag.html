Filipino Spaghetti

Description

Many of you may wonder: "Why is it called Filipino Spaghetti? And what is this urge to know more?" Well, buckle up buckaroo because you're in for a wild ride.

Filipino spaghetti does indeed share some common ingredients with a tomato based pasta dish, perhaps a ragu. You have your pasta, tomato sauce, onion, garlic, ground beef, and some salt to taste. But this is where the commonalities end. Things start to take a turn with the addition of processed cheese, Filipino ketchup (i.e. banana sauce), red hot dog slices, and sugar. Lots of it.

Those with a discerning palette will understand why this dish has become The culinary classic it is today. So without further ado, I present Filipino Spaghetti.

Ingredients:

  • Pasta
  • Tomato sauce
  • Ground beef
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Banana sauce
  • Processed cheese block
  • Neutral oil
  • Sugar

Steps:

  1. Heat neutral oil on medium in a deep sauce pan
  2. Dice onions and garlic, slice red hot dogs
  3. Once oil shimmers, introduce onions and garlic
  4. When onions become translucent, add ground beef
  5. When ground beef is cooked through, add tomato sauce
  6. Allow mixture to simmer
  7. Bring salted water to a boil
  8. Add pasta and cook until al dente
  9. Taste meat sauce and add sugar, salt, and pepper to taste
  10. Drain pasta and place into serving container
  11. Top pasta with sauce
  12. Top sauce with shredded cheese and hot dogs
